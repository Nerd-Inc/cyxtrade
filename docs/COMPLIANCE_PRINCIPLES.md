# CyxTrade Compliance Principles

> CyxTrade is built for legitimate remittances and fair currency exchange, not anonymity for crime.

---

## Why This Document Exists

CyxTrade is a permissionless protocol, but permissionless does not mean lawless.

This document defines what CyxTrade stands for, what it rejects, and how abuse is deterred and enforced against.

---

## What CyxTrade Is Aiming For

CyxTrade aims to deliver:
- Low-fee remittances for underserved corridors
- Access for users excluded by legacy rails
- Non-custodial trade security through smart-contract bonds
- Community-based dispute resolution with staked arbitrators

CyxTrade does not exist to "avoid government agencies." It exists to avoid centralized custody and single-point gatekeepers while supporting lawful use.

---

## What CyxTrade Does Not Promote

CyxTrade does not promote, support, or tolerate:
- Money laundering
- Terrorism financing
- Sanctions evasion
- Fraud, scams, and impersonation
- Human trafficking or other criminal payments
- Tax evasion and corruption payments

Note: the correct term is money laundering (not "money laundry").

---

## No Hiding Place for Bad Actors

Bad actors should not find safe harbor in the CyxTrade network.

Core reasons:
- Economic stake: traders lock bonds that can be slashed
- Transparent records: trade state transitions are auditable
- Dispute evidence trail: evidence is submitted, reviewed, and linked to case outcomes
- Human accountability: arbitrators are staked and penalized for corrupt behavior
- Progressive limits: tiering, cooldowns, and volume caps constrain abuse velocity
- Repeat-offender controls: suspension, ban lists, and restricted re-entry pathways

Principle statement:
- There is no hiding place for bad actors on the CyxTrade network.

---

## Lawful-Use Commitment

CyxTrade deployment should be corridor-aware and jurisdiction-aware. Operational policies should include:
- AML/CFT controls proportionate to risk
- Sanctions screening where applicable
- Suspicious activity escalation and review
- Record retention aligned with legal requirements
- Cooperation pathways for valid legal process

---

## Enforcement Model

CyxTrade enforcement combines protocol rules and operations:

1. Prevent
- Bond requirements
- Tier gating and trade limits
- Velocity controls and cooldowns

2. Detect
- Pattern-based risk signals
- Dispute analytics
- Repeated counterparty complaints

3. Respond
- Trade locks and temporary restrictions
- Arbitration and evidence review
- Bond slashing, victim compensation, suspension, and bans

4. Recover and harden
- Post-incident policy updates
- Rule tuning by corridor risk profile
- Ongoing monitoring for re-entry abuse

---

## Messaging Guidelines

Use this language:
- "Non-custodial, lawful remittance infrastructure"
- "Trust-minimized exchange with economic accountability"
- "Built for legitimate users in underserved corridors"

Avoid this language:
- "Unstoppable anonymous transfers"
- "No government can touch this"
- "Use CyxTrade to bypass regulation"

---

## Non-Negotiables

- User protection over growth hacks
- Accountability over anonymity for malicious behavior
- Auditability over opaque decision making
- Compliance-ready operations over regulatory arbitrage
